# **THE TOXICOLOGIST'S HANDBOOK**

### *A Compact AD\&D Poison System (Deadly, Flexible, Lore‑Grounded)*

*(Inspired by AD\&D DMG poison rules and classic Dragon Magazine poison overhauls, notably issue #81 which expands delivery types, addresses antidotes, and provides deeper mechanics for ingestive, insinuative, contact, and gas poisons.*)\* [\[dndbeyond.com\]](https://www.dndbeyond.com/forums/dungeons-dragons-discussion/dungeon-masters-only/235499-can-anyone-tell-me-some-important-things-about)

## **1. Delivery Types**

(AD\&D traditionally recognizes ingestive, insinuative, contact, and gas poisons.) [\[worldanvil.com\]](https://www.worldanvil.com/w/forgotten-realms-djverne12/a/triboar-settlement)

**Ingested:** Mixed with food/drink; hardest to detect. Often slow, still harmful on a successful save.  
**Insinuated:** Injury-based; coated on blades, needles, traps. Fast, potent, typically 1–2 effective hits before evaporation.  
**Contact:** Absorbed through skin. Extremely dangerous; no effect through heavy gloves.  
**Vapor:** Inhaled. Instant onset; affects an area for a brief duration.  
**Dual‑Vector (optional):** Rare alchemical poisons effective both ingested and insinuated.

***

## **2. Poison Stat Block**

Every poison uses the same quick-read template:

    *   Delivery type(s)
    *   Rarity
    *   Cost per dose
    *   Onset time
    *   Saving throw modifier
    *   Effect on save
    *   Effect on fail
    *   Handling risk
    *   Shelf life
    *   Antidote (if known)

This structure follows the detailed frameworks presented in Dragon’s major poison articles (such as issue #32, which expanded poison classifications into organized groups). ## **3. Handling, Shelf Life, & Detection**

These details are consistent with the expanded considerations in Dragon Magazine #81, such as evaporation rates, antidotes, and missing DMG coverage of contact/gas poisons. [\[Storm King...hunder.pdf \| PDF\]](https://ametekinc-my.sharepoint.com/personal/wayne_taylor_ametek_com/Documents/Microsoft%20Copilot%20Chat%20Files/Storm%20King%27s%20Thunder.pdf)

### **3.1 Coating Weapons**

    *   1 dose → 2 small weapons *or* 1 medium weapon.
    *   2 doses → 1 large weapon.
    *   Hit #1: full effect.
    *   Hit #2: half effect (evaporation begins).
    *   Hit #3+: inert.
    *   Improper handling: 5% chance of self‑poisoning (Assassins ignore this).

### **3.2 Shelf Life**

    *   Injury poisons: \~2 days before degradation (Dragon articles note blade venoms evaporate rapidly). [\[worldanvil.com\]](https://www.worldanvil.com/w/forgotten-realms-djverne12/a/triboar-settlement)
    *   Ingested/contact: 1–4 weeks.
    *   Vapors: must be freshly prepared.

### **3.3 Detecting Poison**

    *   Unskilled: 1-in-6
    *   Herbalist/alchemist: 3-in-6
    *   High‑quality variants impose –1 to –3 on detection.

***

## **4. Onset & Saving Throws**

Poison severity ties closely to onset time, matching traditional AD\&D categories and Dragon’s emphasis on poison timing. [\[worldanvil.com\]](https://www.worldanvil.com/w/forgotten-realms-djverne12/a/triboar-settlement)

### **4.1 Onset Times**

    *   *Instant:* Vapors
    *   *Fast:* 1–6 segments (common for contact/injury)
    *   *Moderate:* 1–6 rounds
    *   *Slow:* 1–6 minutes (ingested)
    *   *Delayed:* 1–6 hours (rare assassinations)

### **4.2 Save Modifiers**

    *   +2 : Mild
    *    0 : Standard
    *   -1 : Strong
    *   -2 : Deadly
    *   -4 : Lethal

***

| --------------- | ---------------- | --------------------- |

# **7. Antidotes**

Dragon #81 emphasizes antidotes as an underdeveloped area in AD\&D rules, which this system remedies. [\[dndbeyond.com\]](https://www.dndbeyond.com/forums/dungeons-dragons-discussion/dungeon-masters-only/235499-can-anyone-tell-me-some-important-things-about)

**Universal Antitoxin (100 gp):** +2 to next save (if taken within 1 minute).  
**Specific antidotes (50–200 gp):**

    *   Before onset ends: automatic save (+2).
    *   After onset: halts further effects but not prior damage.

# **8. Crafting & Market Rules**

### **Qualified Poison‑Makers**

    *   Assassins
    *   Bards (setting‑dependent)
    *   Toxicologists / Poison‑Herbalists
    *   Master Alchemists (but culturally taboo)

### **Difficulty Class**

*   Simple 12 | Standard 15 | Deadly 18 | Lethal 20

### **Botches**

*   Fail by 5+: accidental exposure (save +2).
*   Fail by 10+: full exposure (normal save).

***

# **9. Poison Use in Play**

*   Poison is feared; possession often illegal.
*   Bards, assassins, and spies may access higher‑grade toxins.
*   Monsters use natural venoms; adapt stat blocks accordingly (e.g., per AD\&D venom guidance: fast‑acting, often fatal). [\[worldanvil.com\]](https://www.worldanvil.com/w/forgotten-realms-djverne12/a/triboar-settlement)
