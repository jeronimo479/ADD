Claugiyliamatarĺs (Old Gnawbone) Lair in the Deeping Cave

This used to belong to a Green Gragon, refer to The Lost Tomb of the Dwarven Prince.

It is now rumored to be controlled by the Mind Flayer, 

KryptGarden Forest inhabitted by Dryads, satyrs and other sylvan creatures make the forest their home along with a clan of goblins.

Green Dragon Mask was stolen.

The Alhoon ValĺXootiz claimed Claugiyliamatarĺs lair.

Find the cave. Recon the area. Return to Triboar with information.

5x 2nd level players

Perplexity:


## The Deeping Cave
This version assumes the cave is a **small, dangerous reconnaissance dungeon** for 5 level-2 PCs, with the alhoon as an offstage threat rather than a fair combat target. The design goal is to let the party uncover that **ValĺXootiz has taken over the lair, but not to force a lethal showdown**. The adventure also supports the rumor that the **Green Dragon Mask** has been stolen and may be hidden deeper in the site or already moved elsewhere.

## Adventure Map
Use a simple coordinate system with the cave entrance at **A1**.

### Surface and Approach
- **A1. Hidden Sinkhole Entrance.** A shallow ravine masked by overgrowth, steep muddy sides, and old green-stained stone. Search DC equivalent: careful inspection reveals claw marks and old dragon-scrape gouges.
- **A2. Watch Vantage.** A collapsed pine and stone outcrop overlooking the approach. This is where goblin scouts observe traffic.
- **A3. Tangled Briar Verge.** A patch of thorn and moss conceals an old side trail used by dryads and satyrs.
- **B1. Forest Trail Split.** One path leads to goblin territory, the other to a ôsafeö route that the fey can reveal.
- **B2. Blighted Ring.** A circle of dead grass where psionic contamination has sickened the plants.

### Cave Level 1
- **C1. Narrow Descent.** A sloping tunnel with slick green mineral deposits.
- **C2. Goblin Pick Post.** A crude barricade and lookout chamber.
- **C3. Offerings Niche.** Bones, spoons, feathers, and stolen trinkets placed before a half-elf skull shrine.
- **D1. Dragon-Ancient Hall.** Old lair architecture: vaulted stone, claw grooves, soot stains.
- **D2. Collapse Chamber.** A cracked room partly blocked by rubble, with a hidden crawlspace.
- **D3. Silent Pool.** A still underground pool with reflective black water.
- **E1. Thrall Barracks.** Goblins sleep here under compulsion and fear.
- **E2. Rotted Archive.** Moldy records, maps, and broken scroll tubes.
- **E3. False Hoard.** A decoy treasure room meant to lure intruders.
- **F1. Ritual Chamber.** The alhoonĺs working room, cold and unnaturally clean.
- **F2. Hidden Reliquary.** The place where the Green Dragon Mask may be kept.
- **F3. Escape Tunnel.** A narrow outlet leading into root-choked bedrock.

## Wilderness Encounters
These work for the trek through Kryptgarden Forest.

### 1. Dryad Warning
A dryad named **Lethrien** watches from a tree line and warns the party that ôthe cave has become hungry in a different way.ö She will not fight unless attacked, but she knows a hidden path to A3. If aided or respected, she reveals that goblins have been bringing offerings inland.

### 2. Satyr Mislead
A satyr troupe offers music, wine, and a ôshortcut.ö This shortcut is real, but it passes near B2 and includes a hidden hazard. If the party jokes, sings, or plays along, the satyrs will share that ôthe dead one in the cave hates bright iron and holy words.ö

### 3. Goblin Scout Patrol
Three goblins and one goblin leader are searching for trespassers. They are nervous, poorly armed, and more interested in warning their masters than dying for them. They might flee, surrender, or bargain if cornered.

### 4. Blighted Grove
A patch of dead moss and blackened roots surrounds a small stone marker. Anyone spending time here hears faint whispered thoughts; this is the first sign of the alhoonĺs psionic presence.

## Creature Stats
These are **2E-style simplifications** you can use at the table.

### Goblin Scout
- **AC:** 6.
- **HD:** 1-1.
- **HP:** 4 each.
- **THAC0:** 20.
- **Attacks:** Short sword or shortbow.
- **Damage:** 1d6 melee or 1d6 ranged.
- **Morale:** 7, but 5 if leader falls.
- **Special:** Prefers ambush and retreat.

### Goblin Leader
- **AC:** 5.
- **HD:** 1+1.
- **HP:** 7.
- **THAC0:** 19.
- **Attacks:** Short sword, dagger.
- **Damage:** 1d6 or 1d4.
- **Morale:** 8.
- **Special:** Carries a whistle for alerts.

### Giant Rat
- **AC:** 7.
- **HD:** 1-1.
- **HP:** 3.
- **THAC0:** 20.
- **Attacks:** Bite.
- **Damage:** 1d3.
- **Morale:** 5.
- **Special:** May carry disease if you want to make the cave harsher.

### Shadow-Marked Thrall Goblin
- **AC:** 6.
- **HD:** 1-1.
- **HP:** 5.
- **THAC0:** 20.
- **Attacks:** Club or dagger.
- **Damage:** 1d4.
- **Morale:** 10, but only because of magical compulsion.
- **Special:** Fights until dropped; avoids direct eye contact; weak to turning and clerical intimidation.

### Alhoon ValĺXootiz
Do **not** use this as a fair fight for level-2 PCs. Present him only if the party is clever, hidden, or extremely unlucky.

- **AC:** 0.
- **HD:** 10+3.
- **HP:** 43.
- **THAC0:** 11.
- **Attacks:** 2 claws.
- **Damage:** 1d4/1d4.
- **Special Attacks:** Psionics, spell-like dominance, fear aura, life-draining touch if you want to make him terrifying.
- **Special Defenses:** Undead immunities, magic resistance if desired, cannot be reasoned with easily.
- **Morale:** 12.
- **Tactics:** Uses thralls, concealment, and psychic pressure rather than open battle.

## Keyed Locations
### A1. Hidden Sinkhole Entrance
The entrance is concealed by moss, hanging roots, and collapsed stone. A careful approach reveals a faint draft and the smell of damp earth mixed with something old and sour. If the party enters carelessly, they may trigger loose rocks or alert scouts below.

**Trap:** The outer lip can collapse if too many characters crowd it. Anyone on the edge must save or slip 10 feet into mud and stones.

### C2. Goblin Pick Post
Two goblins hide behind a low wall of stacked stones. One watches, the other tends a kettle of roots and fungus. They have a horn and will signal if threatened.

**Encounter twist:** If the party comes from the forest side and speaks calmly, the goblins may assume the PCs are allied with the fey or with ValĺXootiz and will try to bluff rather than fight.

**Treasure:** 13 cp, 8 sp, a bone charm, and a crude sketch of the cave showing ôcold roomö and ôbig skull hole.ö

### D1. Dragon-Ancient Hall
This hall shows the original grandeur of the lair. Old green mineral streaks mark where dragon acid once burned the floor. The walls are carved with old, half-eroded scale patterns and draconic motifs.

**Puzzle element:** The hall has three worn stone plaques bearing a riddle in draconic:
- ôWhat feeds the lair, yet is never eaten?ö
- Answer: **Greed**.
Speaking the answer aloud causes a hidden door to unlock. If the players do not know draconic, the question can be inferred from carvings of hands taking and hoards accumulating.

### D2. Collapse Chamber
This room is partially collapsed. A crawlspace behind the rubble leads to E2, but it is too small for armored movement unless someone is willing to crawl.

**Hazard:** Shifting rubble can bury or pin a character if they rush through it. This is also a good place for a concealed goblin watcher.

### D3. Silent Pool
The water here is black and still. It reflects more than the room contains, showing fleeting images of the surface forest and sometimes a skull-faced figure.

**Puzzle:** If a silver coin, a spoken prayer, or a drop of holy water is offered, the pool reveals a brief vision: ValĺXootiz entering the lair beneath a green-glowing mask case. This is a clue, not a combat scene.

**Creature:** 1 giant rat may lurk in the drainage grating.

### E1. Thrall Barracks
This room is cramped, filthy, and heavily guarded by fear rather than steel. Several goblins here are under magical or psychic coercion. They can be freed by threats, promises, or breaking the controlling focus elsewhere in the cave.

**Encounter:** 4 shadow-marked thrall goblins, but they do not all fight to the death if the party offers a route out.

**Loot:** A bent bronze ring, 2 torches, a sack of stale bread, and a tin token stamped with a stylized eye.

### E2. Rotted Archive
This chamber contains ruined ledgers, water-damaged maps, and fragments of old records. One half-burned scroll mentions the ôgreen lordĺs maskö and a ôbreach beneath the root-stairs.ö

**Clue:** A marginal note in goblin hand says: ôDo not go near the cold mirror unless ValĺXootiz calls.ö

**Treasure:** A scroll case containing a partially legible map fragment and 1 usable spell scroll if you want a reward for searching.

### E3. False Hoard
This is a trap chamber designed to bait greedy intruders. It contains:
- Tarnished goblets.
- Copper coins.
- Fake gems.
- A few real items mixed into the false wealth.

**Trap:** Disturbing the central chest releases a cloud of spores or a sleep-inducing narcotic. One hidden skeleton or ghoul-like guardian can rise if you want a harder version.

**Treasure:** 80 gp worth of mixed coin, a carved jade bead, and a real ring hidden beneath false gold.

### F1. Ritual Chamber
This is the heart of the intruder occupation. The room is kept unnaturally clean. A dark circle has been etched into the floor, surrounded by candles made from black wax and fat.

**Encounter:** 1-2 goblin attendants, plus psychic residue that causes unease. The alhoon is not here unless you want a brief terrifying appearance.

**Clue:** The ritual was used to locate, sense, or attune to the Green Dragon Mask.

### F2. Hidden Reliquary
A concealed stone panel opens to a narrow chamber lined with old green velvet and iron hooks. The reliquary may contain:
- The **Green Dragon Mask**, if you want it still present.
- Or an empty stand, proving it has already been moved.
- Or a decoy mask, cursed to mislead thieves.

**Puzzle:** The panel opens only when the chamber is unlit and three touches are made in the correct sequence on carved dragon scales: head, claw, wing.

**Trap:** If forced, the panel releases a cloud of choking dust and a low-level alarm through the lair.

### F3. Escape Tunnel
This root-choked exit leads out to a ravine or another hidden patch of forest. It is ideal for the alhoon or goblins to escape if the PCs trigger the alarm.

## Magic Items
### Green Dragon Mask
Treat this as a dangerous relic, not a simple treasure.

**Suggested powers:**
- Grants resistance to poison.
- Once per day, allows a minor acidic breath effect or cloud of vapor.
- Gives advantage-like situational bonus on dealings with green dragons, if such a thing is relevant.
- **Cursed clause:** Prolonged use causes obsessive greed, whispering thoughts, or susceptibility to draconic influence.

### Jade Bead of Verdant Whispering
A small treasure from the false hoard. Once per day, it allows the bearer to ask one yes/no question of forest spirits, but the answer comes cryptically.

### Ring of the Hidden Trail
A worn silver ring that grants the ability to ignore one difficult woodland track or hidden path per day.

### Scroll of Lesser Restoration
Useful if you want to reward the party for surviving psionic or undead pressure.

## Puzzle Set
### The Greed Riddle
In D1, the party must answer the old draconic plaque about what feeds a lair but is never eaten. The answer is **greed**.

### The Scale Panel
The reliquary at F2 opens only when three dragon-scale carvings are pressed in order. The pattern is head, claw, wing.

### The Silent Pool Vision
The pool in D3 gives a useful clue only if the party approaches peacefully and makes an offering. It is a test of caution, not combat.

## Running the Alhoon
ValĺXootiz should feel present without becoming a level-2 wipe machine.

Use him as:
- A distant whisper in the barracks.
- A psychic pressure behind doors.
- A silhouette glimpsed through a crack.
- A voice speaking through a thrall.

If you want a direct encounter, make it a **brief interruption**, not a fight: he appears, speaks, and withdraws behind goblins, magic, or a concealed exit.

## Return to Triboar
The best ending is a **reporting mission** rather than a victory parade. The party returns with:
- A cave map.
- The identity of the current occupier.
- Evidence of the alhoon.
- A clue about the Green Dragon Mask.
- A rough estimate of enemy strength and defenses.

Their report can then launch a second adventure: a rescue, a heist, or a strike against ValĺXootiz once the party is stronger.

## Optional Twists
- The goblins are not loyal to ValĺXootiz; they are trapped by fear and can become allies.
- The Green Dragon Mask is already gone, and the cave is only a staging ground.
- A dryad knows a secret entrance into F2 that bypasses most defenses.
- The cave contains a second, older hoard chamber from Claugiyliamatarĺs original tenure.

## Party Guidance
For 5 level-2 PCs, the adventure should have:
- 3 to 5 minor combats total.
- 2 to 4 meaningful investigation scenes.
- 1 major reveal.
- No required boss fight with the alhoon.

The cleanest success condition is **not slaying the inhabitant**, but **getting out with solid intelligence**.
