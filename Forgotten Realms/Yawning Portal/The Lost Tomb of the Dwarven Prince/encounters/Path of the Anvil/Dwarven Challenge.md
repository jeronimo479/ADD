# The Steadfast Crossing

At the far end of the chamber, a tunnel gapes open—seven feet wide and
twelve feet tall, its sheer walls vanishing into darkness as it slopes
downward, curving gently to the right. The floor drops a foot below the
room’s threshold and descends at a shallow angle, its surface coated in
slick grease that gleams faintly in the light, treacherous and nearly
impossible to stand upon. The tunnel floor itself is a hazard—studded with
cruel three-inch spikes and broken at intervals by taller, seven-inch
blades that jut upward like teeth, ready to punish any misstep.

From the ceiling, seven feet above the floor, a narrow rod descends from
the ceiling of the the tunnel, bearing a circular target that faces the
entrance like an unblinking eye. Farther in, barely visible in the gloom
some twenty feet ahead, a second rod extends in the same fashion, its own
target hanging in silent wait.

Carved into the wall is: "Step once, and do not falter. Grimthölm grants
passage only to those who do not break faith with their stride."

> If the players punch the target (AC 8) with enough force (3 dmg) it will
> swing away and cause Grimthölm to pour from many holes in the ceiling
> which will drain onto the floor. It will drain away in 3 rounds. The ooze
> is thick and will not support any weight unless it is violently struct,
> such as caused by boots when running or stomping.
>
> If the players run across the surface they will stay afloat; otherwise
> they will sink to the bottom, and then fall over onto the spikes and take
> d2 damage if they fail vs. wand.
>
> If they do not activate the Grimthölm and try to cross the 100 foot 
> greased floor, they must roll d20 + 12 (or 20) <= DEX or fall, for every
> 5 feet crossed.


# The Burden of Stone

You step into a room with a square hole leading into an open area. The
perfectly circular stone silo is forty feet across. A pair of narrow, 
slick, iron rails runs straight across the chasm and gleam treacherously —
clearly the only way to cross to the far side. On your side is a small
trolley with an open 4' tall steel clamshell surrounding a bench press. On
the press facing you, is the skeleton of a dwarf with it's skull smashed
in, but its hands are still wrapped around the handle of a dwarven war
hammer that was jammed into a slot in the cart's floor.

Carved into the wall is: "Hold steady, the Mountain does not flinch."

> Laying down on the press board, with arms pulling the handles and legs
> pushing the plate, the character can safely cross. Releasing the handles,
> open the protective clam shells and exposes the player to fall rocks.
> Save vs. paralyzation or release the handles. The kick board requires 5
> good strength rolls. Failed strength roll causes trolley to slip back
> toward the start by leg press.


# The Hammered Ascent

A slender 3" chain hangs down form the ceiling into the middle of an iron 
platform. In the middle of the platform is a huge stone 'flywheel'. There
are gears and levers connected to the wheel and platform. There is a drive
gear with three protruding levers at 120 degrees apart.

Carved into the wall, "Let your hammer speak until the stone obeys."

> Spinning the drive wheel fast enough and engaging the clutch will cause
> the platform to rise. The wheel must be struct (AC 8) with 5 damage per
> round to keep the platform rising. Party must ascend 30' which take 10
> rounds of consistent pounding.
