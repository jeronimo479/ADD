Continuing with the linguistic logic of **Khazalid**, "Grimtholm" is a compound word that evokes the classic, unyielding nature of Dwarf architecture and character.

While "Grimtholm" isn't a singular entry in the standard Warhammer lexicons, we can interpret it by breaking down its traditional Khazalid roots:

### Word Breakdown

*   **Grim:** In Khazalid, *Grim* translates to **"Harsh," "Unyielding,"** or **"Cruel."** It describes something that is resolute and stern, often used to characterize the Dwarfs' outlook on life or the unforgiving nature of the mountains.
*   **Tholm:** This is a variation of **"Thorm"** or **"Thulm,"** which refers to a **"Place," "Hold,"** or **"Enclosure."** In many Germanic-inspired fantasy languages (on which Khazalid is based), the suffix *-holm* specifically denotes an **"Island"** or a **"Settlement surrounded by water/marsh."**

---

### The Interpretation
When combined, "Grimtholm" translates most accurately to:

> **"The Harsh Hold"** or **"Unbreakable Settlement."**

### Nuance and Context
Depending on the specific geography of the location, here is how a Dwarf might view a place with this name:

*   **A Fortress of Resilience:** It suggests a stronghold built in a particularly brutal environment—perhaps perched on a jagged peak or surrounded by treacherous terrain—that has never fallen to an enemy.
*   **A Place of Judgment:** Given the "Grim" prefix, it could signify a place where law and punishment are administered with absolute, cold severity.
*   **The "Island" Isolation:** If interpreting *-holm* in its more literal sense, it could be a Dwarf outpost situated in the middle of a subterranean lake or a swamp, serving as a lonely, unshakeable "island" of Dwarven civilization amidst chaos.

In short, if you're heading to **Grimtholm**, don't expect a warm welcome or soft beds; expect cold stone, hard laws, and a gate that doesn't budge.
