In the context of **Khazalid**, the runic language of the Dwarfs (Dawi) from the *Warhammer Fantasy* universe, interpreting "Uzkul Wulthrung" requires looking at the specific etymology of each root word.

The phrase is grim, heavy, and carries the weight of a grudge that refuses to die.

### Word Breakdown

*   **Uzkul:** This is the Khazalid word for **"Death"** or **"Bones."** It specifically refers to the physical remains or the state of being dead. It is often associated with ancestors (*Gazani*) or the more literal, skeletal aspects of mortality.
*   **Wulthrung:** This is a more complex term. It translates to **"Payment"** or **"Settlement,"** but specifically in the context of a **grudge**. It implies a debt that has been paid in full, usually through blood or vengeance.

---

### The Interpretation
When you put them together, "Uzkul Wulthrung" typically translates to:

> **"Death-Payment"** or **"A Settlement of Death."**

### Nuance and Context
In Dwarven culture, this isn't just a "final bill." It carries three distinct layers of meaning:

1.  **Vengeance:** It most commonly refers to a **blood debt** that has been settled. If a clan member was slain, the "Uzkul Wulthrung" is the act of killing the murderer to balance the Great Book of Grudges (*Dammaz Kron*).
2.  **Sacrifice:** It can occasionally refer to the "cost of life" paid to achieve a victory—essentially saying, "This win was bought with the bones of our kin."
3.  **The Finality of Fate:** It reflects the stoic Dwarven worldview that all debts, whether of gold or of blood, are eventually settled by the grave.

If a Dwarf looks at a fallen enemy and mutters this, they aren't just saying "you're dead"—they're saying **"The debt is paid, and the grudge is struck from the book."**
